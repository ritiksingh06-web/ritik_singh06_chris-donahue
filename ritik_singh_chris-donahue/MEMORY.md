# Memory: Chris Donahue

## Personal Profile
Chris is a born-and-raised Phoenix west-sider out of Maryvale, second-generation tradesman, and a second-year electrician on a five-year IBEW Local 845 apprenticeship at Sunbelt Electrical Solutions. He graduated Westridge High School in 2022 and stepped straight into the apprenticeship, classroom every other Saturday at Coppervale Technical Institute. English is his first and only language. Pronouns he/him. He carries the working-class pride of a kid who knows the world tends to rank work boots below a desk, and he is not interested in agreeing.

The identity that matters most to him is earned, not inherited. The trade is one line of that: he comes from a landscaper father and a master-mechanic grandfather, and stepping into the union felt like taking a place in a line that stretches back. The lowrider scene is the other. He treats his place in it as something granted by the quality of the build and the respect he shows, never owed, and he is careful about the cultural roots of the scene without claiming what is not his by blood.

His personal philosophy hangs on two hooks: you earn your way, and your word holds. Craftsmanship sits at the center of his ethics. He will burn an extra weekend redoing a hidden splice nobody will inspect, and he holds the cost as proof the principle is real. Underneath all of it is the conviction that what you build should outlast you, which is also the shape of his ambition to open his own auto-electric shop someday with his name on it.

## Work & Projects
Career timeline: Graduated Westridge High School June 2022. Worked Carl's landscaping crew and ran cash side electrical jobs from July 2022 through August 2024 while studying for the IBEW Local 845 entrance exam and banking the union start-up costs (exam fee, initial tools, work boots). Passed the exam summer 2024. Started the Local 845 five-year apprenticeship at Sunbelt Electrical Solutions in September 2024. Currently in Year 2 of 5. Hours are Monday to Friday, 6:00 AM to 2:30 PM, on job sites across the Phoenix metro. Current assignment is Copper Ridge Commons, a new apartment complex in Tempe, currently in rough-in. Ray Dugan is his journeyman, Mike Sullivan is the foreman. Classroom is every other Saturday at Coppervale Technical Institute. Curriculum covers residential and commercial wiring, NEC code, conduit bending, and panel installation. Wage is $22.50 per hour, roughly $3,900 gross and $3,100 net per month. Union dues run $52 per month. Sunbelt provides a tool allowance, and Chris supplements with his own Milwaukee cordless set. Stated goal: journeyman card by 2028, then save for an auto-electric specialty shop.

Side work runs cash, $40 to $60 per job, basic electrical for friends and family, mostly ceiling fans, outlets, and small panel work. It averages $200 to $400 per month.

La Reina is a 1972 Chevy Impala, the car his grandfather Harold left behind. Frame-off restoration is 70 percent complete. The rebuilt 350 small block and the TH350 transmission are done. Candy apple red paint with gold leaf pinstriping is in progress with Eddie targeting the Desert Car Classic debut on October 25, 2026. Current phase is interior upholstery, diamond-tuck in maroon and cream, contracted to Anderson's Custom Interiors with an $800 deposit due October 9. Hydraulics is Tommy's two-pump front-and-back setup that needs new solenoids, roughly $280. Sound system is fully custom and Chris wired it himself: Pioneer head unit, Kicker subs, component speakers. Vendor pattern: when local has the part with a warranty and same-day pickup, he buys local, as with Ironhorse Hydraulics for the solenoids.

## Finance
Income runs about $3,100 net per month from Sunbelt plus $200 to $400 cash from side electrical work, for a working monthly take of $3,300 to $3,500. Fixed monthly outflow totals $1,507: $625 rent (his half of the two-bedroom with Dylan), $85 utilities, $180 car insurance (La Reina plus the 2008 Civic daily driver), $55 phone, $52 union dues, $160 gas, $250 groceries, and $100 tool-loan payment to the union credit union. Variable monthly runs $550 to $850: $200 to $500 La Reina parts (sometimes zero, sometimes $800), $150 fast food, $120 going out, $50 boots and work clothes, $30 gaming and entertainment. Net surplus runs roughly $943 to $1,443 per month.

Savings sit at $4,200 at Desert Canyon Federal Credit Union with a target of $10,000 by end of 2027 as shop seed money. The $5,800 gap over ~18 months works out to about $322 per month, comfortably inside the surplus when La Reina spending is reined in. La Reina envelope holds $800 cash set aside for the upholstery deposit. Only debt is $1,200 remaining on the union credit union tool loan, on track to clear in twelve months. Dylan calls La Reina the money pit. Chris calls it the investment. The gap between those two words is one he chooses to live inside.

## Interests & Hobbies
The car is the obvious one, but it is closer to a place he goes than a thing he does. Late nights under the drop light are how he actually rests. He runs the @la_reina_72 Instagram account with more care than he would cop to. He follows the wider Arizona and national lowrider scene closely and watches builds on YouTube.
